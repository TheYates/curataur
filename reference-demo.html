Welcome back to the channel. Today we're talking about how to build a budget that actually works, even if your income changes every month.

The first step is figuring out your real average income. Don't use your best month. Use the average of your last six months.

Once you know that number, split your expenses into three buckets: needs, wants, and savings. Needs are rent, groceries, and utilities, and they should never be skipped.

Wants are things like eating out, subscriptions, and entertainment. These are the first to cut when money gets tight. Savings covers your emergency fund, debt payoff, and any investing you're doing.

A simple starting ratio is fifty percent needs, thirty percent wants, and twenty percent savings.

But if your income is irregular, build a buffer account. Keep one month of expenses sitting there at all times. That buffer is what protects you during slow months, so you're not scrambling to cover rent.

Track everything for at least thirty days before you decide your final numbers. You'll be surprised where your money actually goes. Review your budget every month, since your numbers will shift.
